Following These Norms?

Masking

Masks should be used as part of measures to suppress transmission of COVID-19 however, the use of masks alone is insufficient to protect oneself against COVID-19. The appropriate use, storage and disposal of masks is also essential.

How to Wear a Mask:

  • Make sure that your mask covers your nose, mouth and chin. Never use masks with valves.
  • Wash masks regularly and thoroughly if its a fabric/cloth mask and dispose medical masks.
  • Always wash your hands before wearing your mask.

Doctors also recommend double masking - preferably using a fabric/cloth mask and a surgical mask to ensure better protection. If you can, it's also recommended to wear a face shield on top of your mask.

In addition to wearing masks, take simple precautions such as:

  • Social distancing and avoiding crowds.
  • Keeping rooms well ventilated.
  • Washing your hands with soap.
  • If meeting someone is necessary, then do it in an open space.
  • Make sure to wear a mask in the lift, since this is a common hotspot to spread the virus.
  • Try to use toothpicks to press buttons in common spaces.

Scientific experiments have shown that airborne transmission is possible and the virus can rise upto a height of 10ft. Thus, it is essential to stay in well-ventilated rooms/open spaces, avoid large crowds and closed spaces(lifts).

Basics of hygiene:

  • Avoid touching your eyes, mouth and nose frequently.
  • Clean and disinfect surfaces regularly especially those that are frequently touched.
  • Regularly clean your hands thoroughly with a sanitiser or with soap and water.

Steps to take when you are feeling unwell:

  • Self-isolate at home even if you are facing minor symptoms such as cough or fever. For a list of symptoms, refer to COVID-19 Trivia. It is important to know the full range of symptoms. In addition, keep up to date with the improvements in your condition.
